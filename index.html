THRENODY
THE ENDLESS DEEP

For nine hundred years the Threnody held the Deep shut — one endless lament, sung beneath the world on a harp of bone, that kept the dead asleep. Then the Harp was stolen.

Thessaly, once the keep's own songkeeper, broke the song to wake what it silenced. He carries the Harp ever downward; with every floor he descends, the note unravels and the dark climbs one step closer to the surface.

Three go down after him. If they fail, the Deep comes up.
